Second Language Learning, Immersion, and Virtual Reality (SLIVR)

An old and well-theorized concept in second language studies, immersion has suddenly gained currency with the popularity of virtual and augmented reality technologies (VR/AR) and their promises of providing "immersive" experiences. The SLIVR working group I lead at Vanderbilt's Center for Second Language Studies explores the applications of VR and AR in the foreign language classroom, seeking to understand how these technologies complicate our notions of language immersion, and how long-standing debates about immersion in SLS can inform critical conversations about the "new" immersive environments. We pursue two basic goals: developing lessons that bring VR and AR to the FL classroom, and exploring the theoretical implications of FL notions of immersion on digital immersion, and vice versa.
